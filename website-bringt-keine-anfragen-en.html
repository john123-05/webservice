If your website doesn't bring any requests

The website is online, but it is not working properly

Many company websites look neat, but they don't lead visitors to a decision. This is precisely when there are too few inquiries, even though there is a fundamental need.

  • Unclear message on the homepage
  • Too little trust or evidence
  • Mobile user guidance is too weak
  • Contact channels are hidden or unnecessarily complicated

Where most websites lose requests

Often the problem is not just a lack of traffic, but rather that visitors do not understand quickly enough why they should inquire here.

That's why this problem page is intentionally tailored to companies that feel like their website is there but isn't doing enough business-wise.

Frequently asked questions

Is it always the design?

No. Often the real problem is message, trust, structure or contact management.

Do you need a complete relaunch immediately?

Not always. Sometimes targeted adjustments are enough, sometimes a relaunch makes more sense. That depends on the initial situation.

Is this also a SEO topic?

Yes, partially. A website may not bring in enough inquiries because it is either not visible enough or because it does not guide visitors well enough after the click.
